Carbon accounting methodology

This dashboard displays information about your organization's Scope 1 and Scope 2 greenhouse emissions.

Scope 1 emissions are produced from the direct burning of fossil fuels, such as the natural gas typically used to heat buildings. Scope 1 emissions are calculated by multiplying a standard factor by the total amount of fuel used.

Scope 2 emissions are indirect emissions caused by the purchase of energy, most commonly in the form of electricity.

This dashboard uses the location-based method for calculating Scope 2 emissions. The location-based method uses average emissions factors for your regional grid based on the location of your electricity use. These factors are multiplied by your electricity use to determine total Scope 2 emissions. Emissions factors change over time, based on the mix of generation sources on your regional grid.

For electricity use in the United States and some neighboring regions in Mexico, we use the eGRID factors published by the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency and updated roughly annually.

For electricity use in other areas, we use factors published by The Climate Registry.

Emissions are reported in terms of CO2 equivalent (CO2e), a measure that converts greenhouse gasses into their equivalent amount of carbon dioxide based on relative global warming potential.
